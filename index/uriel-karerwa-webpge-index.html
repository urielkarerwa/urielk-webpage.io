Who am I?

At the heart of it all, deep in my bones there's a love for storytelling. I think we're all storytellers, with rich lives that we can translate into stories to share with others. Storyelling is disarming, natural and timeless. A process as old as language. Anyways, here's my story

Where my Journey begins

The story I'm chosing to share starts near the end of my university studies. I spent 5 years studying neuroscience at the University of Lethbridge, learning from some of the most important minds in the field of neuroscience. I gained experience in research, and developed a neuroscientists work ethic. In my final year I was on the dean's honor role for outstanding marks. This is where it all started, where I developed my curiosity and creativity.

Public storytelling

To cap my formidable years studying neuroscience I was able to share my story on the TEDx stage. The theme for TEDx uleth was I am still learning and shared my story about how I made learning more personal, and in the process uncovered many forgotten events from my childhood.

Radio Broadcasting

While doing public radio is when I discovered how much I enjoy sharing in other's stories, struggles and life experiences. In three short years contributing to my public radio station I was able to

  • Host 2 radio shows
  • Interview over 20 guests ranging from musiciens to scientists and comedians
  • Run a podcast
  • Succesfully DJ an event
  • Accrue over 150 hours in radio and podcasting
    • Recorded over 50 hours of spoken word content for radio and podcast
    • Nominated and won the volunteer lethbridge excellence award
  • Honorable mention at the Naitonal Community Radio Award
